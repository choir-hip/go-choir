# Choir Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-06-28
**Service:** Choir (https://choir.news)
**Operator:** Choir

This Privacy Policy explains what data Choir collects, how it is used, who
processes it, how long it is kept, and the rights you have over your data. It is
written in plain language. Where a technical term is needed, we explain it.

Choir is a news synthesis platform. It fetches content from public sources
(RSS feeds, Telegram channels, GDELT, Polymarket, and public web pages),
deduplicates and synthesizes that content using large language model (LLM)
providers, and publishes synthesized articles. The platform also provides a
personal computing surface where authenticated users can create, edit, and
version documents (called "textures") and run agent-based workflows.

## 1. Data We Collect

### 1.1 Source Captures (Public Content)

Choir fetches content from public sources on a recurring basis:

- **RSS feeds:** article titles, summaries, links, and publication dates.
- **Telegram channels:** public channel messages and metadata.
- **GDELT:** global event data from the public GDELT dataset.
- **Polymarket:** public prediction-market data.
- **Public web pages:** content fetched when a source link is followed for
  deeper reading.

This content is public. It is stored in Choir's versioned database (Dolt) for
deduplication, synthesis, and article generation. Each source record carries
provenance metadata: the source URL, fetch timestamp, content hash, and the
source's terms-of-service class, robots policy, and body-storage policy where
available.

We do not knowingly capture content from paywalled, login-required, or
private sources without explicit operator configuration.

### 1.2 User Account Data

When you create an account, we collect:

- **Email address:** used for account identification and communication. Email
  addresses are stored in the auth database. In application logs, emails are
  replaced with a short non-reversible hash (first 8 hex characters of
  SHA-256) so that raw email addresses do not appear in log files.
- **WebAuthn passkey credentials:** cryptographic credential data generated by
  your device's passkey manager (platform authenticator). Choir stores the
  public key and credential metadata. **Choir never stores passwords.** The
  authentication system uses WebAuthn passkeys exclusively — there is no
  password database and no password hashing.
- **Refresh session tokens:** hashed tokens used to maintain your login
  session. Tokens are hashed before storage; the raw token is only ever
  returned once (as a cookie) and is never stored directly.
- **Account recovery tokens:** hashed, single-use tokens used for email-based
  passkey account recovery. Recovery tokens are stored hashed, expire after a
  short period, and are not included in public API responses.

### 1.3 User Content

If you use Choir's personal computing surface, you may create and edit
documents (textures), run agent workflows, and interact with the prompt bar.
This content is stored as versioned artifact state in Dolt. Every revision is
retained for history, diff, and audit purposes.

### 1.4 Trace Events (Observability Data)

Choir records trace events as its primary observability layer. Trace events
capture:

- **Tool calls:** which tools were invoked, with what arguments, and what
  results were returned.
- **LLM I/O:** prompts sent to LLM providers and responses received. This
  includes the content of source captures and synthesized text that was
  processed.
- **Agent-to-agent messages:** structured updates between agents in the
  runtime (findings, evidence, blockers, questions).
- **Run lifecycle events:** submission, start, progress, completion, failure,
  cancellation, compaction, and retry events.
- **Browser session events:** when web browsing is used for source inspection,
  navigation and control events are recorded.

Trace events are persisted to Dolt (the same versioned database that stores
articles and user content). They serve as the system's causal ledger and are
the prerequisite for self-learning, supervision, and auditability.

**PII in trace events:** The production readiness plan includes a PII
retraction pipeline that redacts personally identifiable information from
trace events before persistence, using a local small language model (SLM).
Until that pipeline is operational, trace events may contain raw content from
source captures, including text that was present in public sources. We are
working to close this gap.

### 1.5 Usage Data

We collect basic usage data necessary to operate the service:

- **Authentication logs:** login, logout, and session events (with hashed
  email, not raw email).
- **Cycle logs:** ingestion cycle runs, source fetch results, dedup outcomes,
  and article publication events.
- **Cost tracking:** per-cycle and per-article LLM API cost data (aggregate,
  not user-attributed for the public news service).

We do not use third-party analytics, advertising trackers, or SaaS log
export services. Observability data stays in Choir's own infrastructure.

### 1.6 Email Communications

If you interact with Choir via email (e.g., replying to a notification or
approval request), the email content and metadata are processed by our email
provider (Resend) and ingested into Choir for handling. Email metadata (sender,
recipient, subject, timestamp) and body content may be stored as part of the
agent workflow that processes the reply.

## 2. How Data Is Used

### 2.1 News Synthesis

Source captures are used to:

- Detect and deduplicate news items across sources (using content-hash
  deduplication and semantic deduplication via vector embeddings).
- Synthesize articles that combine and contextualize information from
  multiple sources.
- Generate published articles on choir.news.

### 2.2 Semantic Deduplication

Source content is converted to vector embeddings using a local embedding model
(Ollama) and stored in Qdrant, a vector database. These embeddings are used to
detect semantically similar items and avoid publishing duplicate coverage. The
embeddings are derived from public source content, not from user personal data.

### 2.3 LLM Processing

Source content, user prompts, and agent workflow context are sent to LLM
providers for:

- Article synthesis and generation.
- Deduplication decisions.
- Document editing and revision (for the personal computing surface).
- Agent reasoning and tool-use during workflows.

See Section 3 for details on third-party LLM processors.

### 2.4 Observability and Self-Learning

Trace events are used to:

- Monitor system health and detect failures.
- Provide the supervision hierarchy with structured observations.
- Enable the self-learning layer to improve system behavior over time.
- Audit and replay system state at any point in time (the trace is a
  tamper-evident causal ledger).

### 2.5 Authentication and Session Management

WebAuthn credential data and session tokens are used solely to authenticate
you and maintain your login session.

## 3. Third-Party Processors

Choir uses third-party services to process certain data. These processors
operate under their own privacy and data retention policies.

### 3.1 LLM Providers

Source content, user prompts, and agent context are sent to LLM providers for
synthesis and processing. The current set of LLM providers includes:

- **AWS Bedrock (Anthropic models):** Claude models accessed via AWS Bedrock.
  Data is sent to AWS for inference. AWS Bedrock's data privacy terms apply.
- **Z.AI:** GLM models accessed via Z.AI's Anthropic-compatible API.
- **DeepSeek:** DeepSeek models accessed via OpenAI-compatible and
  Anthropic-compatible APIs.
- **Xiaomi (MiMo):** MiMo models accessed via OpenAI-compatible and
  Anthropic-compatible APIs.
- **Fireworks AI:** Kimi and DeepSeek models accessed via Fireworks' OpenAI-
  compatible API.
- **ChatGPT / OpenAI:** Models accessed via OpenAI's API for certain agent
  roles and tasks.

**What is sent to LLM providers:**

- Source capture text (titles, summaries, and fetched body content from
  public sources).
- User prompts and instructions (when using the personal computing surface).
- Agent workflow context, including prior tool results and document state.
- System prompts and configuration data.

**What is not sent:**

- Your WebAuthn credentials or password data (none exist).
- Your session tokens.
- Your email address (except where needed for email-based workflows).

**Provider data retention:** Each LLM provider has its own data retention and
training policies. Choir configures providers to avoid data retention for
training where the provider offers that option, but we cannot guarantee that
all providers honor zero-retention requests. You should assume that content
sent to LLM providers may be retained by those providers according to their
own policies. We disclose the providers used so you can review their policies:

- AWS Bedrock: https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/
- OpenAI: https://openai.com/privacy/
- Anthropic: https://www.anthropic.com/privacy
- Z.AI: review their applicable privacy terms
- DeepSeek: review their applicable privacy terms
- Fireworks AI: https://fireworks.ai/privacy

### 3.2 Email Provider (Resend)

Resend processes inbound and outbound email for Choir. Email content,
metadata, and delivery status are handled by Resend according to their privacy
policy: https://resend.com/privacy.

### 3.3 Embedding Model (Ollama, Local)

Vector embeddings for semantic deduplication are generated by a local Ollama
instance running in Choir's infrastructure. Source content is sent to this
local service only; it is not transmitted to a third party.

### 3.4 No Advertising or Analytics Trackers

Choir does not use third-party advertising networks, marketing trackers, or
SaaS analytics services (such as Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or Segment).
Observability data remains in Choir's own infrastructure.

## 4. Data Retention

We retain data for the following periods. These are our default policies; we
may adjust them as the system matures.

| Data type | Retention period | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Source captures (raw) | 90 days after last reference | Dedup, audit, and reprocessing |
| Source captures (metadata) | 1 year | Source health monitoring and provenance |
| Published articles | Indefinite (versioned in Dolt) | Published content is the product |
| User textures / documents | Indefinite while account is active | Versioned artifact state; full revision history |
| Trace events (raw) | 90 days | Observability and debugging |
| Trace events (aggregated) | 1 year | Derived metrics and long-term analysis |
| Auth credentials (passkeys) | Until account deletion or credential removal | Required for authentication |
| Refresh sessions | Until expiry or logout | Session management |
| Account recovery tokens | Until expiry or shortly after use | Account recovery and abuse prevention |
| Authentication logs | 90 days | Security audit |
| Email ingestion data | 90 days | Workflow processing and audit |
| LLM provider data | Controlled by provider policy | See Section 3.1 |

**Dolt time-travel:** Dolt is a versioned database. Even after data is
"deleted" from the current working state, historical versions may remain
accessible through Dolt's time-travel feature until explicitly purged. We
commit to purging deleted-user data from Dolt history as part of our erasure
procedure (see Section 6).

**Qdrant vectors:** Vector embeddings derived from deleted source content or
user data are removed from Qdrant as part of the erasure procedure.

## 5. Data Security

### 5.1 Authentication

- **No passwords:** Choir uses WebAuthn passkeys exclusively. There is no
  password database, no password hashing, and no password reset flow.
  Authentication relies on your device's platform authenticator (Touch ID,
  Face ID, Windows Hello, or a hardware security key). Email-based account
  recovery may be used to register a replacement passkey.
- **Passkey storage:** Choir stores only the public key portion of your
  passkey credential. The private key never leaves your device.
- **JWT-based sessions:** Access tokens are short-lived JWTs signed with
  Ed25519. Refresh tokens are hashed before storage.

### 5.2 Cookies

Choir uses two cookies for session management:

- `choir_access`: a short-lived access token (JWT) used to authenticate
  requests.
- `choir_refresh`: a longer-lived refresh token used to obtain new access
  tokens without re-authenticating.

Both cookies are:

- **HttpOnly:** not accessible to JavaScript, preventing cross-site scripting
  (XSS) theft.
- **SameSite=Lax:** provides baseline cross-site request forgery (CSRF)
  protection.
- **Secure:** transmitted only over HTTPS in production.

Choir does not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or third-party
analytics cookies. No cookie-based tracking or profiling occurs.

### 5.3 Transport Security

All communication with choir.news is over HTTPS (TLS). Internal service
communication is being audited to ensure encryption between all components
(source cycle service, runtime, Qdrant, Dolt, Ollama).

### 5.4 Credential Management

LLM provider API keys, Telegram bot tokens, and other secrets are stored as
environment variables or in secrets management infrastructure. They are never
committed to the repository, baked into build artifacts, or exposed to the
browser or guest environments.

### 5.5 Data Isolation

User data is stored in per-user embedded Dolt workspaces. The auth database
stores user identity and credentials separately from content and trace data.

## 6. Your Rights (GDPR and General Data Protection)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or another
jurisdiction with data protection laws, you have the following rights:

### 6.1 Right of Access

You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. This includes
your account data, textures/documents you have created, and trace events
attributable to your account. To make a request, contact us at the address in
Section 10.

### 6.2 Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

You can request that we delete your personal data. We will:

- Delete your account, email, and WebAuthn credentials from the auth
  database.
- Delete your textures, documents, and artifact state from Dolt.
- Remove your data from Dolt version history (time-travel purge).
- Remove vector embeddings derived from your data from Qdrant.
- Delete trace events attributable to your account.

**Limitations:** We may need to retain certain data where required by law, or
where data has been anonymized/aggregated such that it no longer identifies
you. Published articles that were synthesized from public sources are not
considered your personal data and will not be deleted as part of an erasure
request, unless the article contains your personal data.

**LLM provider data:** We cannot guarantee deletion of data that has already
been sent to LLM providers. We will request deletion from providers where
their policies allow, but provider-side retention is governed by their own
terms.

### 6.3 Right to Data Portability

You can request an export of your personal data in a machine-readable format.
This includes your account information, textures/documents (with full revision
history), and relevant trace events. Exports are provided in JSON and/or
Markdown format.

### 6.4 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your data based on consent (particularly LLM processing of
content you submit through the personal computing surface), you can withdraw
consent at any time. Withdrawing consent will stop further processing but
will not affect processing that has already occurred.

### 6.5 Right to Object

You can object to certain types of processing, particularly processing for
the self-learning layer or observability that involves your content. We will
assess whether the processing is necessary for legitimate interests or legal
compliance.

### 6.6 Right to Rectification

You can request correction of inaccurate personal data. For textures and
documents, you can edit them directly through the product surface.

### 6.7 Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection
authority if you believe we have not handled your data properly.

## 7. International Data Transfers

Choir's infrastructure is hosted in the United States. LLM providers may
process data in different jurisdictions:

- **AWS Bedrock:** data may be processed in AWS regions in the United States
  or other regions.
- **OpenAI:** data processed in the United States.
- **Anthropic (via Bedrock):** data processed according to AWS regional
  policies.
- **Z.AI, DeepSeek, Xiaomi, Fireworks:** data may be processed in their
  respective operating jurisdictions, which may include countries outside the
  EEA.

When your data is transferred to a country outside your jurisdiction, the
legal basis for the transfer depends on the provider's data transfer
mechanisms (such as Standard Contractual Clauses). We disclose the providers
used so you can assess the transfer risk. If you are uncomfortable with a
specific provider's jurisdiction, contact us to discuss whether we can
configure your processing to avoid that provider.

## 8. Children's Privacy

Choir is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly
collect personal data from children under 16. If you believe a child under 16
has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will take steps
to delete that data.

## 9. Consent for LLM Processing

By using Choir's personal computing surface (prompt bar, texture editing,
agent workflows), you acknowledge that the content you submit may be processed
by third-party LLM providers as described in Section 3.1. This processing is
necessary to provide the service.

For the public news service (reading articles on choir.news), no
user-submitted content is sent to LLM providers. Source captures from public
sources are processed by LLM providers for synthesis, but this does not
involve your personal data.
